Why this layout works for McDonough in May:

  • Pole beans + cucumbers on north trellis — vertical = more bed real estate, won't shade smaller plants. Beans fix nitrogen for next season.
  • Zucchini gets 4 squares — it sprawls, but gives you 30+ fruits per plant in Georgia summer.
  • Okra + eggplant — both thrive in 90°F+ heat when tomatoes start to suffer. Long harvest window: July through October.
  • Bush beans + cowpeas in front — short, easy to pick, fix nitrogen. Cowpeas (southern peas) replace your bolting sugar snaps.
  • Carrots between cowpeas — carrots root deep, cowpeas root shallow. No competition.
  • Marigolds at corners + nasturtiums in front — nematode and aphid deterrents. Nasturtium flowers are edible.
  • Basil tucked next to tomato area — repels tomato hornworm. (Plus pesto.)

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Kids' Patch Ideas

Esme, Violette & Judah — instant-gratification crops.
  • Cherry tomatoes — they pick straight off the vine, eat like candy.
  • Sunflowers — sow a row along bed edge, mark each kid's name on a stake. Whose grows tallest?
  • Strawberries — start in a container ("kid bucket"). Everbearing varieties produce June-October.
  • Mini pumpkins — sow late July for Halloween harvest. Vines sprawl — needs a corner of yard.
  • Nasturtium flowers — edible, peppery. Kids love picking and eating flowers.
  • Carrots — pulling them out is the most satisfying garden moment for any kid.
